I've designed websites for university departments, literary journals, academic journals, presses, and classes. I match aesthetics to a site's function, typically emphasizing usability. The sites here are made with a combination of CSS3, HTML, and jQuery. I have extensive experience with Wordpress and SharePoint.

Vannevar is an essay site built using Wordpress. It is designed to give multiple users access and provide them with easy to use WYSIWYG options when updating the site. It is also responsive and features an extensive use of Wordpress plug-ins.

The CU-Boulder Human Resources site runs on Microsoft SharePoint. The branding was designed to match that of the main campus design (a Drupal site). The design not only serves the public portion of the site, but also the back end and various webparts, calendars, folders, and libraries, that reside on the server.

Timber is a literary journal run by the MFA students at the University of Colorado-Boulder. Because of the site's purpose, the text is displayed prominently on the main page. Wordpress is used as a CMS, and permissions have been designed to accomodate a rotating editorial staff.

This website functioned as an evolving syllabus for a creative writing course. It was personally managed and used CSS3 and HTML5 exclusively.

Robot Melon is an online literary journal founded in 2003. Early issues were designed using Dreamweaver, but all issues since #11 have been created using CSS3 and HTML5.

English Language Notes is a scholarly journal based in the English Department at CU-Boulder. This site is a reiteration of a previous design. It has been converted from a Dreamweaver template to Wordpress, which functions as a CMS for the site and gives editors easier access to page updates.

Subito Press is a literary publisher of fiction and poetry. The site was designed with usability in mind for a rotating staff. Wordpress is used as a CMS. The design was conceived from scratch, including the header and other site images.
